It's Wednesday. The holiday weekend starts Friday. Here's everything you need to know across every major sports story happening right now, in the time it takes to read this.

## Wimbledon (Day 2 underway)

The Championships opened Monday and the second round is underway. The first two days of Wimbledon always produce the most lopsided results of the tournament — top seeds navigating the opening rounds while first-time Wimbledon participants adjust to the grass — but Day 1 already produced the upsets that always come in the first round.

**Who to watch this weekend:** The second round ends by Thursday. Third round begins Friday and Saturday, which is when the seeded players start encountering real competition. The Wimbledon weekend draw typically produces the best tennis of the first week: more balanced matchups, players who've found their grass-court rhythm, and the first indications of who's genuinely dangerous in the bracket.

**Where the streaming is:** Peacock and ESPN+ split the coverage depending on match and court. Centre Court matches during prime daytime hours (UK afternoon, US morning) land on ESPN+. Peacock carries some of the outer courts and later-round coverage. Checking both before settling in is the practical move.

**The bottom line:** You don't need to watch every match. Watch the third-round matches on Saturday. That's when the tournament's actual shape becomes clear.

## Tour de France (Stage 4 today, mountains starting this weekend)

The Tour started last Saturday and five days in, the race is still in its early stages — flat terrain through northern France, sprinters winning stages, GC riders saving their legs and staying out of trouble. The early GC standings don't mean much yet; the gaps that determine the winner of the Tour open in the mountains.

**When it gets interesting:** The first summit finishes arrive in the Pyrenees this weekend. That's when the Pogačar vs. Vingegaard battle actually starts. Stages with summit finishes are the ones worth watching — or at minimum, worth catching as a 10-minute highlight package from the Tour's YouTube channel.

**Weekend stages to watch:** Any stage that ends at altitude. Check the stage profile before deciding whether to follow live. Flat stages through the weekend can be skipped; summit finishes cannot.

**The bottom line:** The Tour is warming up. The weekend's mountain stages are the first real test. If you're going to engage with the Tour at all this summer, start with Saturday or Sunday's stage depending on the profile.

## World Cup 2026 (Knockout round in progress)

The group stage is complete and the knockout bracket is live. Sixteen teams remain. The 2026 World Cup is co-hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico — which means matches are spread across venues in all three countries, and the US home crowd factor is real for the teams playing on American soil.

**This weekend's fixtures:** The round of 16 is running through early next week. Check the full schedule — there are matches Thursday, Friday, and Saturday, and the quality of knockout round football is categorically different from group stage. Every team knows elimination is on the line.

**The storylines that matter:** Canada playing on home soil at this World Cup is historically significant — their program's resurgence over the past cycle brought them here, and the crowds in Canadian venues have been among the tournament's most intense. Mexico's knockout tournament record at World Cups has historically been strong through the first round. The US team, on home soil in a co-hosted tournament, is playing in front of the biggest crowds in tournament history.

**The bottom line:** Watch at least one knockout round match this weekend. The stakes transformation from group stage to knockout is dramatic. Pick the most compelling fixture and watch it live — this round doesn't produce draws.

## MLB (At the midseason turn)

The All-Star break is in roughly two weeks, which means this is the stretch where the trade deadline narratives start forming. Every team's record is now meaningful — the contenders are identifiable, the sellers are identifiable, and the teams in the middle of that conversation are where the interesting storylines live.

The holiday weekend slate is a full one. July 4th weekend is historically one of MLB's higher-attendance weekends because the travel patterns align with family gatherings that include baseball games. Night games on July 4th with fireworks afterward are a specific American tradition worth experiencing at a local ballpark if you have one accessible.

**The bottom line:** For the holiday weekend, MLB is background noise unless you follow a specific team in a pennant race. Check your team's standings position and whether they're in the buyer or seller conversation as the deadline approaches.
