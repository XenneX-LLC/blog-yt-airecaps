Happy 4th. You're at a cookout, or about to be, and you don't want to disappear into your phone all day — but you also don't want to miss what's happening. Here's the whole sports landscape in one scroll, so you can check it once and get back to the grill.

## World Cup: Round of 16 Is Live Today

Today's slate includes multiple round of 16 matches, and this is genuinely the best day to check in if you've only been half-following the tournament. Knockout football has no group-stage safety net — a loss today means the team is out, which changes how every match plays. Check the bracket, pick the matchup with the storyline you care about most (a host nation, a rivalry, a team with a star player), and catch the highlights if you can't watch live.

The three co-host nations — US, Canada, Mexico — are all still very much part of the tournament conversation this week, and the crowd energy at their matches has been some of the most intense of the tournament.

## Wimbledon: Third Round Weekend

Wimbledon's third round lands this weekend, which is when the tournament actually starts separating the contenders from the field. First week matches always produce some surprises — that's true again this year — and third round is where the seeded players who survived the opening rounds start meeting real competition.

If you check one match today: find whichever third-round match has the most compelling storyline (a rising player, a rivalry, a seed facing an unseeded opponent who's been playing well) and catch the highlights tonight if you can't watch live during the cookout.

## Tour de France: Stage Today, Mountains This Weekend

The Tour is still in its early flat-terrain phase. Today's stage is unlikely to produce major GC drama — that starts this weekend when the race hits the Pyrenees. If cycling isn't your thing, today is a skippable stage. If you're planning to follow the Tour at all, bookmark this weekend instead.

## MLB: Full Slate, Fireworks After

Nearly every MLB team plays today, and July 4th is one of the traditional fireworks-night dates across the league. If you're not near a stadium, most local team broadcasts are airing today's games, and the between-innings energy on a 4th of July broadcast is always a little different — more flag ceremonies, more patriotic moments, sometimes a genuinely great game to go with it.

## The One-Glance Version

If you only have 30 seconds before someone hands you a plate: check the World Cup bracket for today's knockout matches, note whether your team (if you have one) is still in Wimbledon, and know that the Tour de France gets interesting this weekend, not today.

That's the whole sports landscape. Now go enjoy the cookout.
